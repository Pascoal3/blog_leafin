Seasonal Gardening Checklist: What to Plant Month by Month

Gardening isn't just a spring activity.
It's a year-round journey.
Each month offers unique opportunities.
To plant, harvest,
And prepare your garden For success.
Whether you're a beginner.
Or an experienced green thumb.
This month-by-month gardening checklist will keep your garden thriving.
Through every season.

🌱 January – Prepare and Plan

January is the perfect time to dream.
And design.
While the soil might be frozen.
Your creativity shouldn't be.

  • Review last year's results: What worked? What didn't?
  • Clean tools and storage spaces
  • Order seed catalogs and start building your wishlist
  • Plan your crop rotation and garden layout

🛠️ Pro Tip: Start a garden journal.
It'll keep your notes.
Sketches, and timelines.
In one place.

🌿 February – Start Indoors

February is all about seed-starting indoors.
And getting ahead start on the growing season.

  • Start seeds for tomatoes, peppers, and herbs under grow lights
  • Check your soil and compost supply
  • Prune dormant trees and shrubs
  • Force bulbs like hyacinths and tulips for indoor color

🌟 Get ahead of pests: Install netting or covers before bugs arrive.

🌸 March – Sow Cool-Season Crops

When spring is nearly here, and it's time to get your hands dirty!

  • Direct sow peas, lettuce, spinach, and radishes
  • Transplant hardened seedlings started indoors
  • Clean up garden beds and remove mulch or covers
  • Start composting again actively

Sharp blades and a comfortable grip reduce fatigue and improve accuracy.

🌼 April – Full Swing Spring

April is the ideal month for planting and expanding.

  • Plant potatoes, carrots, and onions
  • Start warm-season crops indoors (zucchini, melons)
  • Fertilize established perennials
  • Divide and transplant overgrown plants

🌿 Don’t forget flowers! Add marigolds or nasturtiums to deter pests naturally.

🌞 May – Garden in Bloom

Now’s the time for serious planting.

  • Transplant tomatoes, peppers, and cucumbers
  • Mulch garden beds to conserve moisture
  • Stake or support growing plants early
  • Keep an eye on weeds and pull them while they’re small

✅ Check weather updates: A late frost can ruin young transplants!

🌞 June – Maintain and Mulch

Things are heating up.
Your job now is to maintain.

  • Water deeply and consistently
  • Monitor for pests like aphids and caterpillars
  • Deadhead flowers to encourage blooms
  • Harvest early crops like lettuce and spinach

🐝 July – Harvest and Feed

Summer is in full swing.
And your garden is alive.

  • Harvest beans, tomatoes, and herbs
  • Feed flowering plants and heavy feeders
  • Shade tender from harsh afternoon sun
  • Top off mulch where needed

✅ Check weather updates: A late frost can ruin young transplants!

🍂 August – Succession and Prep

Don't stop now.
Fall gardening begins here.

  • Plant fall crops like kale, broccoli, and carrots
  • Dry herbs and collect seeds from maturing plants
  • Trim back spent flowers
  • Plan cool-season crops

✅ Check weather updates: A late frost can ruin young transplants!

🐝 July – Harvest and Feed

Summer is in full swing.
And your garden is alive.

  • Harvest beans, tomatoes, and herbs
  • Feed flowering plants and heavy feeders
  • Shade tender from harsh afternoon sun
  • Top off mulch where needed

✅ Check weather updates: A late frost can ruin young transplants!

🍁 September – Preserve and Plant

A transitional month perfect for preserving. and preparing.

  • Preserve your harvest (canning, drying, freezing)
  • Plant cover crops to improve soil
  • Start bulb planting (garlic, daffodils)
  • Refresh compost pile

🍃 October – Protect and Clean

Time to wind down the growing season.

  • Remove dead plants and compost them
  • Add mulch to protect perennials
  • SDrain hoses and store tools
  • Plant trees and shrubs - they’ll establish over winter

🌧️ November – Reflect and Repair

November is quiet but important.

  • Clean and oil tools
  • Cover garden beds to protect soil
  • Organize seed storage
  • Reflect on your gardening year

❄️ December – Rest and Research

Let your garden rest.
And take time to grow as a gardener.
Come back here to check the trends.
And to learn more.

  • Research new gardening techniques
  • Read gardening books or blogs
  • Attend a local workshopor join online gardening groups
  • Dream big for the next season!

And keep your garden on track all year long!
