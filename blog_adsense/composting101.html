What You Can Compost

Composting starts in your kitchen and garden. Here are safe materials to toss into your compost bin:

‚úÖ Greens (Nitrogen-rich)

  • Fruit and vegetable peels
  • Coffee grounds
  • Fresh grass clippings
  • Tea bags (without plastic)

‚úÖ Browns (Carbon-rich)

  • Dried leaves
  • Shredded paper or cardboard
  • Sawdust
  • Egg cartons

Remember: The ideal compost pile has a balanced mix of greens and browns ‚Äî roughly a 1:3 ratio.

üõë What Not to Compost

Some items can attract pests or create harmful bacteria.

‚ùå Avoid:

  • Meat, bones, or dairy
  • Oily or greasy foods
  • Pet waste
  • Diseased plants

Every seed you plant is a step toward a better planet.

üõ†Ô∏è How to Start Your Own Compost

You don't need fancy equipment. Just follow these steps:

1. Choose a Location

  • Outdoor: A shaded, well-drained spot works best
  • Indoor: Use a composting bin with a carbon filter to avoid odors

2. Build Your Layers

  • Alternate between brown and green layers to keep the balance.

3. Turn Regularly

  • Aerate the pile weekly to speed up decomposition.

4. Wait & Harvest

  • Between 2 and 4 months, your compost will be dark, crumbly, and smell like soil. That's garden gold.

üåü Pro tip: Start small. Success with a single plant builds confidence and excitement.

Why should every gardener compost?

  • Adds essential nutrients without chemicals
  • Suppresses plant diseases naturally
  • Encourages healthy root growth
